Protect your data!


The Transit secrets engine in HashiCorp Vault handles cryptographic functions on data in-transit. Vault doesn't store the data sent to the secrets engine. It can also be viewed as "cryptography as a service" or "encryption as a service". The Transit secrets engine can also sign and verify data, generate hashes and HMACs of data, and act as a source of random bytes.




The primary use case for Transit is to encrypt data from applications while still storing that encrypted data in some primary data store. This relieves the burden of proper encryption/decryption from application developers and pushes the burden onto the operators of Vault.


The Transform secrets engine in HashiCorp Vault handles secure data transformation and tokenization against the provided secrets. Transformation methods encompass NIST vetted cryptographic standards such as format-preserving encryption (FPE) via FF3-1 to encode your secrets while maintaining the data format and length. In addition, it can also be pseudonymous transformations of the data through other means, such as masking.




Think of a scenario where an organization must cryptographically protect the personally identifiable information (PII) while preserving the data format and length. For example, the database schema expects a certain character length and/or only allow alphanumeric. The preservation of the original data format or length may be driven by compliance with certain industry standards such as HIPAA or PCI. This prevents the need for change in the existing database schema.


In this demonstration, we'll be using transit to protect Personally Identifiable Information (PII). It is also used to protect other sensitive data.
